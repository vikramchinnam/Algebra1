When Ones Make 10 or More…

Sometimes when we add the ones, we get 10 or more.
That's too big to fit in the ones place!

So we bundle 10 ones into 1 new ten and carry it over to the tens column.
We call this regrouping — it just means moving a group to the right column. 🎁

🦊 Think of it like this:
You have 14 stickers. You put 10 in a bag (= 1 ten) and keep 4 loose (= 4 ones).
So 14 = 1 ten + 4 ones.
Three magic rules:
  1. Always add the ones first.
  2. If the ones total is 10 or more, write the ones digit and carry the 1.
  3. Add the tens — don't forget to include the carried 1!

💡 Example 1

27 + 15 = ?

¹  2 7 + 1 5
 4 2
  1. Add the ones: 7 + 5 = 12
  2. 12 has 2 ones and 1 ten — write 2 in the ones place.
  3. Carry the 1 to the tens column (the little "¹" on top).
  4. Add the tens: 2 + 1 + 1 (carried) = 4 — write 4 in the tens place.
  5. ✅ Answer: 42

💡 Example 2

38 + 26 = ?

¹  3 8 + 2 6
 6 4
  1. Add the ones: 8 + 6 = 14
  2. 14 = 4 ones + 1 ten — write 4, carry the 1.
  3. Add the tens: 3 + 2 + 1 (carried) = 6 — write 6.
  4. ✅ Answer: 64
🌟 Notice the pattern: Whenever your ones add up to 10 or more, you always carry exactly 1 to the tens. The leftover ones go in the ones place.

🧭 Guided Practice

Hints are ON for these 3 problems. Take your time! 😊

Problem 1 of 3 Direct
46 + 37 = ___
💡 Hint: Add the ones first: 6 + 7 = 13. Write 3, carry 1. Then add tens: 4 + 3 + 1 = ?
Problem 2 of 3 Missing Number
29 + ___ = 54
💡 Hint: Think backwards — 54 − 29. Ones: 4 needs more ones, so borrow. Or: what plus 29 gives 54?
Problem 3 of 3 True or False
57 + 34 = 91  →  Is that TRUE or FALSE?
💡 Hint: Add ones: 7 + 4 = 11. Write 1, carry 1. Tens: 5 + 3 + 1 = 9. So the answer is 91. 🤔
